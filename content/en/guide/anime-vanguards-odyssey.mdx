<Section icon={Target} title="Step-by-Step Anime Vanguards Odyssey Route">

Successfully navigating an Odyssey run requires a methodical approach to deployment, upgrading, and card selection. Follow this tested progression path to optimize your runs and secure your rewards.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Establish Your Economy Early">
    Begin the run by placing your cost-reduction or income units, such as Demon Leader. Do not over-invest in expensive damage units during the first few waves. Instead, use cheap, efficient starter units or basic summoners to handle early enemies while you build a stable financial foundation.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Deploy Your Primary Crowd Control">
    Before the first major speed-boosted wave arrives, deploy a crowd control unit like Gujo (Infinity) or Ice Queen. Position them at key intersections or loops where their slows and freezes can impact the maximum number of targets. This establishes a reliable safety window for your carries.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Draft High-Value Scaling Cards">
    As you complete floors and draft cards, prioritize permanent stat boosts such as Damage increases, Range expansions, and SPA reductions. Avoid picking temporary or highly situational buffs unless they directly synergize with your main carry's primary damage type (e.g., Burn or Bleed boosts).
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Deploy and Maximize Your Main Carry">
    Once your economy is secure and the lanes are controlled, deploy your heavy-hitting carries, such as Rogita (Super 4) or God (Above Heaven). Focus your gold on fully upgrading these units one by one, rather than partially upgrading multiple weaker units across the map.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Manage Late-Game Boss Waves">
    During the final boss encounters, activate your units' active abilities manually. Time your freezes or time stops to interrupt devastating boss attacks, and ensure your single-target DPS units are targeting the boss rather than minor trash mobs.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Upgrade Timing">
Never spend all your gold upgrading a single unit if a lane is currently leaking fast enemies. It is always better to drop a cheap slowing unit or summoner to secure the lane before committing to expensive high-tier upgrades.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Traits, Rerolls, and Memoria Overhaul">

The rewards earned from the **anime vanguards odyssey** mode—specifically Trait Rerolls and Memoria Shards—are critical for late-game unit optimization. Traits act as permanent stat modifiers on your units, while the newly overhauled Memoria system in Update 13.5 provides massive passive combat boosts.

### Trait Reroll Pool and Pity Thresholds

When rerolling traits at the Mandra Traits NPC, players should aim for mythic traits on their primary carries. Each attempt consumes 1 Trait Reroll, and the game features a pity system that guarantees rare outcomes over time.

| Fortune | 2.500% | Economy and farming units (increased gold income) | No Pity |

### Update 13.5 Memoria System Changes

Update 13.5 introduced sweeping changes to how Memoria functions, streamlining progression for all players:

- **Evolved by Default**: Memoria evolutions have been completely removed. All Memoria are now evolved by default, and evolving a unit automatically grants its designated Memoria.
- **Memoria Recycling**: Players can now recycle unwanted duplicate Memoria into rarity-based Memoria Scrap, which can be traded in the Exclusive Shop.
- **Leveling Tickets**: Use Memoria Leveling Tickets to instantly boost a newly acquired Memoria to Level 60, bypassing the tedious grinding process.
- **Stat Transfer Support**: Easily transfer high-value stat rolls from older units to your newly acquired meta characters without losing your investment.

<Callout type="success" title="Investment Strategy">
Only spend your Trait Rerolls on units that have already completed their evolution and ascension paths. Rerolling traits on temporary, unevolved units is a waste of rare resources that are better saved for your permanent meta cores.
</Callout>
