<Section icon={Target} title="All anime vanguards banner Types Explained">

To spend your resources efficiently, you must understand the differences between the four primary banner types available in Anime Vanguards. Each banner operates under unique rotation rules, costs, and pity systems.

### Special Banner
This is the default banner in the game. The featured units rotate every single hour, allowing players to target specific characters throughout the day. When a unit is featured on the Special Banner, the unit in the middle has a **50% chance** of being obtained upon hitting that rarity, while the units on the left and right each have a **20% chance**. A single pull costs **50 Gems**, which is discounted to **40 Gems** if you have the VIP gamepass.

### Special Memoria Banner
Introduced in Update 11.0, this banner functions identically to the Special Banner but is dedicated entirely to pulling high-value Memorias. It rotates hourly and shares the same 50/20/20 distribution for middle and side featured items.

### Event Banner
The Event Banner features exclusive limited-time units (such as the Winter or Spring Event units) and requires unique Event Currency instead of standard Gems. VIP discounts do not apply here. These banners feature a unique Vanguard Unit at an incredibly low drop rate, but they also offer a one-time pity mechanic to assist dedicated players.

### Selection Banner
First introduced in Update 6.5, the Selection Banner is a highly player-friendly feature. It allows you to freely select any summonable Mythic unit to occupy the middle spot. This selection persists for **24 hours**, giving you a full day to target-farm a specific meta unit. If you do not choose a middle unit, the side units will continue to rotate hourly as normal.

| Banner Type | Primary Currency | Rotation Cycle | Key Feature |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Special Banner** | Gems (50 base / 40 VIP) | Hourly | Standard unit acquisition with VIP discounts |
| **Special Memoria** | Gems (50 base / 40 VIP) | Hourly | Dedicated banner for pulling powerful Memorias |
| **Event Banner** | Event Currency | Hourly | Features exclusive limited-time Vanguard units |
| **Selection Banner** | Gems (50 base / 40 VIP) | 24 Hours (Middle) | Players choose which Mythic unit is featured |

<Callout type="tip" title="Shared Pity System">
The Selection Banner and the Special Banner share the exact same pity count. You can build up your pity on the Special Banner and unleash it on the Selection Banner once your desired Mythic unit is set in the middle.
</Callout>

</Section>

Pulling on an **anime vanguards banner** requires an understanding of the underlying probability rates. The base drop rates are highly stratified, rewarding lucky players with rare visual and functional upgrades.

### Base Summon Rates
According to the official database [1], the standard probabilities for summoning units and memorias are structured as follows:

| Rarity Class | Base Pull Probability | Level Requirement | Key Characteristics |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Rare** | 75.496% | None | Standard fodder used primarily for selling or basic evolution |
| **Epic** | 20.000% | None | Moderate utility; can occasionally roll as a Shiny |
| **Legendary** | 4.000% | None | Strong mid-game units; guaranteed at 50 summons |
| **Mythic / Exclusive** | 0.500% | None | High-tier meta units; guaranteed at 400 summons |

### Shiny and Serialized Mechanics
When you successfully roll an Epic, Legendary, Mythic, or higher rarity unit, there is a **1.5% base chance** (increased to **3% with the Shiny Hunter Gamepass**) that the unit will be a **Shiny**. Shiny units feature distinct visual aesthetics and can be normalized or transferred.

Furthermore, event-exclusive Vanguard and Secret units have a **0.5% chance** to roll as a **Serialized** unit when they are Shiny. This results in an overall pull rate of approximately 1 in 400 million for a serialized version of specific event units. Serialized units feature:
- A unique serialized number visible upon hovering over the unit.
- A custom 1-of-1 cosmetic model for the first 10 units of that type obtained server-wide.
- Distinct visual model changes and numbering integrated directly into the character design for any serial number above 10.

<Section icon={Shield} title="Pity Mechanics and Auto-Sell Optimization">

To protect players from poor luck, Anime Vanguards implements a robust pity system. This system tracks your summons and guarantees high-rarity drops after a specified number of attempts without success.

### Pity Thresholds
Pity thresholds differ slightly depending on the banner type and the item being summoned:

| Banner Category | Legendary Pity | Mythic Pity | Vanguard Pity | Pity Reset Behavior |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Special & Selection** | 50 Summons | 400 Summons | 25,000 Summons | Resets upon pulling that rarity (except Vanguard) |
| **Special Memoria** | 50 Summons | 400 Summons | 20,000 Summons | Resets upon pulling that rarity (except Vanguard) |
| **Event Banners** | 50 Summons | 400 Summons | 20,000 Summons | Vanguard pity is strictly one-time only |

On Event Banners, the Vanguard Pity is a **one-time guarantee** for players at Level 10 or higher. If you pull the Vanguard unit naturally before hitting the pity threshold, the pity is considered spent and will not refresh for that specific banner cycle.

### Auto-Sell Configuration
To prevent your inventory from filling up with unwanted Rare and Epic units during massive summoning sessions, you should configure the **Auto-Sell** feature. This tool automatically converts selected rarities into Gold immediately upon summoning.

<Callout type="success" title="Auto-Sell Safety Net">
The Auto-Sell feature will **never** automatically sell Shiny units or units that roll with custom Traits. If you wish to automatically sell units that have Traits, you must manually enable that option in the Miscellaneous Settings menu. Shiny units can only be sold manually.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="The Normalize Shop: Shiny Transfer & Normalizing">

Valentine runs the Normalize Shop, situated directly adjacent to the main summon area. This shop provides critical utility for managing your Shiny units.

### Shiny Transfer
If you pull a Shiny version of a unit but already have a highly leveled or evolved normal version of that exact same unit, you can perform a **Shiny Transfer**. 
- This process transfers the Shiny status from the food unit to your primary unit.
- Both units must be the exact same character.
- Each transfer costs **20,000 Gold**.
- **Warning**: The original Shiny unit used as the source will be permanently deleted during the transfer.

### Normalizing and Selling Shinies
If you do not want a unit to remain Shiny, you can choose to **Normalize** it. Normalizing removes the Shiny status from the unit. In return, the player is awarded **5 trait rerolls**, regardless of the unit's rarity. 

Alternatively, manually selling a Shiny unit from your inventory also rewards you with **5 trait rerolls**. 

| Action Type | Gold Cost | Unit Requirement | Reward / Output |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Shiny Transfer** | 20,000 Gold | Same normal & shiny unit | Transfers shiny status; deletes source unit |
| **Normalize Unit** | Free | Any Shiny unit | Removes shiny status; awards 5 trait rerolls |
| **Sell Shiny Unit** | Free | Any Shiny unit | Deletes unit entirely; awards 5 trait rerolls |

<Callout type="tip" title="Fusing Restriction">
Fusing a Shiny unit into another character during leveling will **not** award you with 5 trait rerolls. Always normalize or sell your unwanted Shiny units manually to secure your rerolls.
</Callout>

</Section>
