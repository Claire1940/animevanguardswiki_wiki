<Section icon={Swords} title="What Can and Cannot Be Traded">

Navigating the trading economy requires a thorough understanding of which assets can be transferred and which must remain on your account [1]. The developers have established strict rules to maintain game balance and prevent the devaluation of active content. 

The primary rule governing unit trading is that only limited units that are completely unobtainable can be traded [1]. If a limited unit is still active or obtainable through current in-game methods, it remains locked to your account [1]. Furthermore, when you trade a qualified limited unit, its level, ascension status, and stat rolls are preserved [1]. However, its trait and pity counter are completely wiped [1]. If your unit possesses an S-rank stat or higher, the exact percentage of that stat is visible to both parties when hovering over the unit in the trade window [1].

In addition to units, players can trade various giftable items and cosmetics [1]. For instance, gamepasses, bundles, and battlepasses purchased with Robux can be safely exchanged [1]. When trading bundles, hovering over the bundle icon displays its exact contents to prevent scams [1]. Trait rerolls can also be traded, but only if they were purchased directly using Robux; rerolls obtained through bundles or in-game rewards are non-tradable [1]. Skins and familiars, whether limited or non-limited, are generally tradable [1]. The sole exception to the familiar rule is Vanguard Familiars, which cannot be traded due to their lack of stat variation, inability to be corrupted, and their status as temporarily limited items between updates [1].

Mounts have also seen significant changes. According to the [Anime Vanguards Wiki](https://animevanguards.fandom.com/wiki/Trading), an update on February 1, 2026, allowed players to convert previously unobtainable bundle mounts into tradable mount items [1]. This includes highly sought-after mounts like the Anime Vanguards Bike, Snow Bike, and Shadow Dragon [1].

<Section icon={Shield} title="Trade History and Trade Bans">

Security is a major component of the trading system. To help players keep track of their transactions and resolve disputes, the developers included a comprehensive History menu [1]. Located at the top right of the trading screen, this menu allows you to view a detailed log of all past trades, including the items exchanged and the names of the other players [1]. You can also use the built-in search bar to filter your history by player name [1].

To protect the integrity of the market, the game employs automated systems to detect duplicated items and currencies [1]. Any player flagged for possessing or distributing duped items is immediately trade banned [1]. These bans are highly restrictive and persist until a staff member manually reviews the case [1]. It is vital to only trade with trusted players and avoid deals that seem too good to be true, as receiving duped items can put your account at risk.
