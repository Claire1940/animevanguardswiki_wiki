<Section icon={Target} title="Core Team Building Principles">

Building a reliable team in Anime Vanguards is not simply about stacking the flashiest Secret or Vanguard units. A functional team needs synergy, cost management, and lane control. Players must account for early-wave defense, steady income generation, crowd control (CC), and high-output boss damage. 

Without an economy unit, players will struggle to afford the expensive upgrade paths of meta carry units during mid-game waves. Similarly, neglecting crowd control will cause fast-moving enemies to slip past defenses on harder Nightmare difficulties. Slot efficiency is also a major factor, which is why summon-based units like Song Jinwu and Igros are highly valued. They create additional blocking units on the path without occupying standard unit placement slots.

| Role | Key Functions | Optimal Units |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Economy / Farm** | Generates gold, lowers deployment costs, stabilizes upgrades | Demon Leader (Control), Fortune-traited farm units |
| **Primary DPS** | Clear dense waves, defeat bosses, apply damage over time | Rogita (Super 4), Alocard (Vampire King), Demon Hybrid |
| **Crowd Control** | Stun, slow, freeze, or hold enemy lanes in place | God (Above Heaven), Gujo (Infinity), Ice Queen (Release) |
| **Summon Support** | Create extra blockers, control lanes, save placement slots | Song Jinwu and Igros, Lich King (Ruler) |

<Callout type="info" title="Slot Efficiency Note">
Vanguard units like Song Jinwu and Lich King generate shadow soldiers directly on the track. These summons draw enemy aggro and deal continuous damage, allowing you to defend multiple lanes without exceeding placement limits.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Shield} title="Mid-Game and Budget Team Progression">

New players cannot immediately summon Vanguard or Secret units. Transitioning from basic starter units to high-tier meta teams requires a step-by-step upgrade plan. The focus during early progression is to clear Story Mode stages to farm Gems and Gold, which are then used to summon stronger units.

Avoid investing heavily in temporary characters. Focus instead on securing budget-friendly Mythic units that offer excellent value for their cost.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Use Starter Units Efficiently">
    Begin with Ichiga, Narutu, or Luffo to clear early worlds. Do not spend Trait Rerolls or Stat Chips on these starters; save your resources for permanent team members.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Secure a Budget AoE Unit">
    Summon Mythic units like Explosive Demon (Bomb) or Cursed Shinobi (Black Flames). Their area-of-effect (AoE) burn damage helps clear packed waves in early story chapters.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Add Mid-Game Utility">
    Bring in Saber (Black Tyrant) or Skele King (Rock) to bridge the gap. These units offer respectable damage and progression scaling without requiring excessive upgrade gold.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Transition to Secret & Vanguard Units">
    Once you accumulate Gems from daily rewards, quests, and active codes, target summon banners featuring meta carries like Rogita (Super 4) or Gujo (Infinity).
  </Step>
</Steps>

| Stage | Recommended Units | Strategy Focus |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Early Story** | Starter Unit, Saber (Black Tyrant), Slime (King) | Keep upgrades cheap, focus on clearing waves fast with basic AoE DPS. |
| **Legend Stages** | Explosive Demon, Cursed Shinobi, Skele King (Rock) | Introduce burn mechanics and utility damage to chip away at higher HP pools. |
| **Mid-Game Bridge** | Savior (Moonless), Okorun (Turbo), Pink Villain | Upgrade to affordable Secret/Exclusive units for speedier resource farming. |
