<Section icon={Swords} title="The Spell Cycling Mechanic & Element Synergy">

The core mechanic of this unit revolves around his four spell slots. He does not attack unless you manually set his spells. When multiple spells are equipped, he cycles through them sequentially: casting slot one, then slot two, then slot three, and finally slot four before returning to the beginning of the queue. 

Because of this sequential cycling, filling all four slots can drastically reduce the efficiency of your most important spells. For example, if you need a specific debuff to remain active on enemies at all times, equipping other spells will delay the re-casting of that debuff, creating a dangerous window of vulnerability.

| Element | Tier | Key Spell | Primary Effect | Recommendation |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Curse | S-Tier | Create Greater Undead | Summons powerful units | Best for raw damage on single paths |
| Passion | S-Tier | Aura of Despair | Massive area debuff | Equip alone for 100% debuff uptime |
| Cosmic | A-Tier | Cosmic Slow | Applies 50% slow for 30s | Excellent utility for fast enemies |
| Unbound | A-Tier | Unbound Haste | Boosts unit attack speed | Use to maximize overall DPS |
| Elementless | A-Tier | Undead Control | Revives killed enemies | Best for multi-path defense |
| Water/Spark/Nature | C-Tier | Stun / Cleanse | Mediocre crowd control | Avoid due to weak current unit synergy |

His spell strength is also heavily dependent on the elemental composition of your active team. To utilize Curse spells, you must bring Curse-aligned units on your team. Similarly, Passion spells require Passion-aligned units. To bypass these strict restrictions, you can utilize Unbound units, which act as wildcards and count toward any elemental requirement, making team building far more flexible.

<Callout type="tip" title="Spell Slot Strategy">
If you are using this unit strictly for crowd control or debuffing, equip only the Passion element spell. This forces him to cast Aura of Despair on every single attack, ensuring constant uptime on the debuff without any cycling delays.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Map} title="Step-by-Step Setup for Single vs. Multi-Path Maps">

Different map layouts require completely different spell configurations. Using the wrong setup can lead to leaked enemies and failed runs. Follow this systematic setup guide to optimize your spell selection based on the map you are playing.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Analyze the Map Layout">
    Identify whether the stage features a single, unified path or multiple branching paths. Single-path maps allow summons to travel efficiently, while multi-path maps split your forces and leave lanes completely undefended.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Configure Your Spell Loadout">
    For single-path maps, select Curse spells like Create Greater Undead and Supreme Summoning to overwhelm enemies with powerful summons. For multi-path maps, completely unequip Curse spells and rely solely on Elementless spells like Undead Control.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Optimize Placement and Upgrade">
    Place the unit near the front of the lane where his attack range can cover the maximum amount of path. Upgrade him to his mid-tier levels to unlock his core stat scaling while keeping your economy balanced for farm units.
  </Step>
</Steps>

On multi-path maps, traditional summons are highly inefficient because they split randomly down different paths. Undead Control solves this problem by reviving defeated enemies exactly where they die, creating localized blockades right on the active lane. This ensures that no path is left undefended, regardless of how many lanes the map has.

<Callout type="success" title="Summon Pathing">
On single-path maps, even a level one summon can easily catch leaking enemies. This makes the Curse element exceptionally safe for solo progression and story mode farming.
</Callout>
