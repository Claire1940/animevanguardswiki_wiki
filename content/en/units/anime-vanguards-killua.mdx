<Section icon={Swords} title="Stats, Upgrade Costs, and Frenzy Mechanics">

When building your Anime Vanguards Killua setup, understanding his stat scaling and upgrade path is crucial for maximizing efficiency in battle. He starts with a base deployment cost of ¥525, which is highly affordable for early-game defense. His total upgrade cost sits at ¥8,725, making him one of the most budget-friendly Epic units to fully max out during a match.

His kit revolves around two unique passives:
1. **Trained Assassin**: Every 8 to 16 seconds, Kinnua enters a frenzy state. During this frenzy, his Seconds Per Attack (SPA) is reduced by 40% for a duration of 7 seconds. This massive speed boost drastically spikes his DPS.
2. **Lightning Strike**: While the frenzy is active, Kinnua gains a +50% chance to stun any enemies hit by his wide-line attacks. This allows him to act as an exceptional crowd-control unit, slowing down fast-moving waves and giving your main damage dealers time to clean up.

<Section icon={Shield} title="Best Synergies & Team Compositions">

While Kinnua is a solid unit on his own, his true potential is unlocked when you build a team around his Spark element. In Anime Vanguards, elemental interactions play a massive role in high-tier content. Kinnua's primary synergy partner is Rukia (or Rukio), who inflicts the 'Absolute Zero' freeze status on enemies.

When a Spark unit like Kinnua attacks an enemy affected by Absolute Zero, the damage effect is repeated. This interaction effectively doubles Kinnua's damage output, turning his decent Epic-tier DPS into a monstrous boss-melting force. To set this up, place your Rukia units slightly ahead of Kinnua so they apply the freeze status first.

In addition to elemental synergy, Kinnua benefits heavily from standard attack and range buffers. Aizen (Eizan) is highly recommended, as his buff provides a 50% damage boost and a 20% range increase. Pairing Aizen with Brooke (Skele King) for his passion attack buff ensures Kinnua's base damage is pushed to its absolute limit. With a full buff cycle and a high-level Memoria, Kinnua's attack range can easily exceed 50, allowing his wide-line lightning strikes to cover the entire map.

| Partner Unit | Role | Key Synergy Effect |

| Rukia / Rukio | Freeze Support | Applies Absolute Zero, doubling Spark damage |
| Aizen / Eizan | Damage & Range | Grants +50% damage and +20% range buff |
| Brooke / Skele King | Attack Buffer | Increases raw attack power with song buff |
| Orihime / Orehimi | Cleanse Support | Cleanses mind control in GGX and boss maps |

<Section icon={Trophy} title="Game Mode Viability and Performance Showcase">

Kinnua has been thoroughly tested across various high-difficulty stages to see how he holds up in the 2026 meta. Here is a breakdown of his performance in different game modes:

- **Seed Raid**: Kinnua absolutely shines here when paired with Rukia. The Lord of the Shadows boss has various immunity phases and annoying mechanics, but the Absolute Zero double-damage combo melts his health bar before these mechanics can stall your run.
- **GGX Stage (Champion Modifier)**: This stage features a final boss that can mind-control your units and has heavy armor stacks requiring 8 individual hits to break. Kinnua's 3-hit wide-line attack makes short work of the armor stacks. By deploying Orihime to cleanse the mind control and staggering your Kinnuas to maintain constant stun-locking, you can clear this stage with ease.
- **AOT EX Stage**: Kinnua handles the giant waves and nuclear titans beautifully. Thanks to his massive buffed range, he can strike targets from across the map, and his high-frequency stuns stop fast-regenerating giants in their tracks.
- **Rifts**: This is where Kinnua meets his match. Rift bosses scale up to 135 million HP, and since Kinnua lacks infinite scaling mechanics like Song Jinwu or Alucard, he will struggle to secure a clear here. However, for an Epic unit, his performance is still highly commendable.
